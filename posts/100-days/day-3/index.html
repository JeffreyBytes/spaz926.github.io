100 Days of Swift: Day 3

March 1, 2019 2 minutes read
100 Days of Swift • Swift • Education
swift • education

Day 3 started out with a quick review of structs and classes, their properties and methods, access control, and optionals. It quickly turned into studying and taking detailed notes about protocols and extensions.

Review

During my time learning Swift I took a deep intrest in the differences between classes and structs, so I fell deep into the rabbit hole early on in my journey to learn as much as I possibly could about them. Access control and optionals I’ve never had much issue with. It’s always good to review the content though and I read up on each one and made sure to test my skills with the review quizzes on Hacking with Swift.

Protocols and Extensions

I never understood much about protocols. I’ve used them in my code — usually because the documentation of the framework I was using told me I needed to (thanks Realm) — but never fully understood what I was doing and why. So today most of my time studying was taken by reading up on this fundamental that has slipped through the cracks for me.

Like protocols, extensions seem to have eluded me during my time with Swift. Thanks to meticulous studying today I feel like I have a good grasp on both of these concepts, and have quiz scores to prove it.

Final Thoughts for the Day

I’m not going to lie. I thought I was going to come into today and breeze through some simple fundamental concepts and be able to play around in Xcode for a while, but almost the entirety of my hour was taken by learning about protocols and extensions. I’m so grateful to Paul Hudson for creating this series because who knows how much longer I would have gone not completely understanding these two things.
